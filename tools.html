Super Visa Insurance Guide
Everything you need to know to bring your parents or grandparents to Canada safely.

What is Super Visa? A multi-entry visa allowing parents/grandparents to stay up to 5 years per visit.

  1. Purchase minimum $100,000 Canadian medical insurance
  2. Get a financial support letter from the Canadian sponsor
  3. Sponsor must meet LICO +30% income threshold
  4. Complete medical exam if required by IRCC
  5. Apply online through IRCC with all supporting documents
  6. Insurance must cover hospitalization, healthcare, and repatriation

New Immigrant Financial Checklist
The 20 most important financial steps in your first year in Canada.
  • Apply for SIN immediately upon arrival
  • Open a Canadian bank account
  • Apply for a secured credit card
  • Register for CRA My Account
  • Apply for provincial health card
  • File your first tax return (even with $0 income)
  • Apply for GST/HST credit and CCB
  • Open a TFSA
  • Get a Canadian driver's license
  • Get life insurance while young and healthy
  • Check foreign credential recognition
  • Research government settlement programs
  • Set up an emergency fund (3 months expenses)
  • Get disability insurance if employed
  • Open RESP if you have children
  • Compare money transfer services (Wise, Remitly)
  • Create a monthly budget
  • Learn about RRSP
  • Make a will and name beneficiaries
  • Check credit score after 6 months (Borrowell)

First Home in Canada Guide
Complete roadmap for buying your first home in Canada.

Key programs for first-time buyers: FHSA ($40K tax-free), RRSP Home Buyers' Plan ($35K), First Home Buyer's Tax Credit ($10K).

  1. Check credit score (aim for 680+)
  2. Open an FHSA and start contributing
  3. Save down payment (5% min for <$500K)
  4. Get mortgage pre-approval (compare 3+ lenders)
  5. Hire a buyer's real estate agent (it's free)
  6. Hire a real estate lawyer ($1,500–$2,500)
  7. Get a home inspection
  8. Budget for closing costs (3–5% of price)
  9. Get home insurance before closing
  10. Get term life insurance to cover mortgage

Credit Score Guide for Newcomers
Build a strong Canadian credit score from zero.

Canadian credit scores range 300–900. You need 680+ for best mortgage rates.

  • Get a secured credit card
  • Use it for groceries/gas only
  • Pay full balance monthly — no exceptions
  • Keep utilization below 30%
  • After 12 months, get unsecured card
  • Check score free monthly at Borrowell
  • Never miss a payment
  • Avoid multiple applications at once
⏱️ Timeline: 680+ in 12–18 months with consistent effort.

RESP — Registered Education Savings PlanRESP — ਰਜਿਸਟਰਡ ਸਿੱਖਿਆ ਬੱਚਤ ਯੋਜਨਾ

The government gives your child 20% on every dollar you save for education. Here's how to capture every dollar of that free grant.ਕੈਨੇਡਾ ਵਿੱਚ ਇਸ ਵਿਸ਼ੇ ਬਾਰੇ ਪੂਰੀ ਗਾਈਡ — ਅਸਲ ਉਦਾਹਰਣਾਂ ਅਤੇ ਕੰਮ ਦੇ ਕਦਮਾਂ ਨਾਲ।

How RESP Grants Work

Every dollar you contribute earns the Canada Education Savings Grant (CESG): — Basic CESG: 20% on the first $2,500/year = $500/year free — Maximum lifetime CESG: $7,200 per child

Additional grants for lower-income families: — Under $53,359/year: Extra 20% on first $500 = extra $100/year — $53,359–$106,717/year: Extra 10% on first $500 = extra $50/year

Canada Learning Bond: Families under $53,359 receive $500 automatically year 1 + $100/year after — no contribution needed.

Long-term math — $2,500/year for 15 years: Your contributions: $37,500 + Grants: $7,200 + Growth at 6%: ~$32,000 = ~$77,000 at age 18. The government contributed ~10% of the final value.

Family vs Individual RESP

Individual RESP: One beneficiary. If child doesn't go to school, grants are returned.

Family RESP: Multiple beneficiaries (must be related by blood/adoption). Funds shared between siblings. If one doesn't pursue education, the other can use everything.

Which to choose: If you have or plan multiple children, always use a Family RESP. The flexibility to share funds between siblings is valuable.

Where to open: Avoid Scholarship Plan companies (Heritage, Canadian Scholarship Trust) — high fees, restrictive rules. Open at Questrade, Wealthsimple, or a direct brokerage to invest in low-cost ETFs.

What RESP Can Pay For

Qualifying programs include: — University and college degrees — Technical and vocational schools — Apprenticeship programs — Recognized international institutions

Three withdrawal types: 1. EAP (grants + growth): Taxed in student's hands — they likely pay minimal tax 2. PSE (your contributions): Withdrawn tax-free, anytime 3. Repayment: If no school, grants returned; growth transferred to RRSP or taxed + 20% penalty

Tax strategy: Have students draw EAPs each year (taxed at their low rate). Keep contributions as a separate withdrawal.

What If Your Child Doesn't Attend School?

What you lose: Grants ($7,200 max) are returned to the government.

What you keep: — All your contributions — back to you, tax-free — Investment growth, with two options: 1. Transfer up to $50,000 to your RRSP (if you have room — avoids penalty) 2. Withdraw: pay income tax + 20% penalty on growth only

Example: You contributed $30,000. Growth = $18,000. Grants = $6,000. — Get $30,000 back (tax-free) — Return $6,000 in grants — Transfer $18,000 growth to RRSP (if room available) = no penalty

The worst-case scenario is still better than not saving at all.

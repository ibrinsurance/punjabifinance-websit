Canada Super Visa: Complete Financial Guide for Sponsors and Parents

The Super Visa allows parents and grandparents to stay in Canada for up to 5 years per visit. Here's everything you need to know financially before applying.

🇨🇦 What Is the Super Visa?

The Super Visa is a 10-year multi-entry temporary resident visa specifically for parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens and permanent residents. Key features: each visit can be up to 5 years (extended from 2 years in 2022), the visa is valid for 10 years (multiple renewals possible), parents can travel back and forth to their home country, and parents are not required to become permanent residents to benefit.

The Super Visa is often preferable to full permanent residency sponsorship because: no 20-year financial undertaking to CRA, parents maintain home country ties and potentially health coverage, faster processing (6 months vs. years for parent PR sponsorship), and no minimum income requirements beyond LICO for the host.

📋 Sponsor Eligibility Requirements

Who can sponsor: Canadian citizens and permanent residents who are the direct child or grandchild of the visiting parent/grandparent.

Income requirement: The sponsor must demonstrate income at or above the minimum necessary income (MNI) — which is LICO × 1.0 (not 1.3× like parent sponsorship for PR). For 2024, minimum incomes vary by household size:
— Household of 2 (you + 1 parent): ~$36,902/year
— Household of 3 (you + spouse + 1 parent): ~$45,351/year
— Household of 4 (you + spouse + 2 parents): ~$55,076/year

Proof: recent Canadian tax return Notice of Assessment and/or current employment letter with annual salary. Self-employed sponsors use NOAs from the past 1-3 years.

Letter of invitation: Required from the Canadian child/grandchild, confirming the invitation and financial support commitment.

🏥 The Mandatory Insurance Requirement

This is the most important financial requirement — and the largest ongoing cost. Every Super Visa applicant must have Canadian private health insurance meeting all of these conditions:

— Minimum $100,000 coverage
— Valid for at least 1 year from the entry date (not application date)
— Must cover emergency medical, hospitalization, and repatriation
— Must be from a Canadian insurance company (not an international policy)
— Must be paid in full or financed with proof of purchase

Annual cost: For a healthy 60-year-old parent: $1,500-$2,200/year. For a 70-year-old parent with stable health conditions: $2,500-$4,500/year. For parents with significant health conditions (diabetes, heart disease, prior cancer): $5,000-$12,000+ annually. Shop between multiple Canadian insurers — rates vary significantly for the same coverage level.

💰 Monthly Financial Costs to Budget

Beyond insurance, budget for these ongoing costs for each visiting parent:

Dental and vision: Not covered by any provincial health plan for Super Visa holders. Budget $1,500-$3,000/year for routine dental and vision care, or purchase supplemental coverage.

Prescription medications: Not covered. Budget $100-$500+/month depending on the parent's medication requirements. Consider having medications shipped from home country (check import rules) or purchasing ahead during visits home.

Transportation: Older parents may not drive. Account for rideshare, taxis, or your time for transportation.

Living costs: If parents will live separately: $1,500-$2,500+/month for a modest 1-bedroom rental in major Canadian cities. If living with you: additional grocery, utility, and household costs of $400-$800/month.

✅ Tips to Reduce Super Visa Costs

Shop insurance early and annually: Get quotes from Manulife, Sun Life, Allianz, Travelance, and independent brokers. Rates vary by 30-50% for the same coverage. Renew shopping every year as your parents age and health changes.

Choose a deductible: A $1,000-$5,000 deductible reduces premiums by 15-30%. If you're comfortable covering the deductible amount in a health emergency, take it.

Stability period clauses: Most Super Visa policies have a stability period (typically 180 days pre-departure) during which pre-existing conditions must be stable to be covered. Understand your parents' policy's stability requirements before their visit.

Consider annual renewals vs. multi-year policies: Some insurers offer 2-year policies — compare vs. renewing annually. Health changes can make single-year flexibility valuable if your parent's condition changes.
